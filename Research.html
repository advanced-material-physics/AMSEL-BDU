Overview

Our research focuses on advanced materials for solar energy applications. From polymers and molecules to nanostructured materials, we investigate fundamental and applied aspects that drive innovation in next-generation photovoltaics.

Polymers and Molecules

Research in polymeric and molecular systems is directed toward the design and synthesis of new organic semiconductors. These materials enable lightweight, flexible, and cost-effective solar devices.

Environmental Stability of PV

Stability is one of the main challenges for real-world deployment of solar technologies. Our work addresses degradation mechanisms caused by moisture, oxygen, temperature, and UV light.

Thin Films, Surface and Interface

Interfaces play a critical role in charge transport and recombination. We investigate thin-film growth, interfacial chemistry, and surface engineering to enhance device efficiency and longevity.

Emerging Solar Cells

Beyond conventional silicon photovoltaics, we study emerging solar cell architectures such as perovskite, tandem, and organic-inorganic hybrid systems.

Nanoparticles and Plasmonics

Nanostructured materials and plasmonic effects are explored to improve light absorption, charge separation, and overall solar device performance.
